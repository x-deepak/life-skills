# Listening and Active Communication

### Q1. What are the steps/strategies for Active Listening? (Minimum 6 points)
- Don’t be distracted by your own thoughts when someone is speaking  
- Don’t interrupt the speaker  
- Show them that you are interested  
- Show that you are listening by using body language  
- Take notes for important information  
- Paraphrase what others have said to make sure you are on the same page  


### Q2. According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening? (Write in your own words, use simple English)

- Key points include focused attention on the speaker without any distractions, empathetic understanding without necessarily agreeing, paraphrasing to confirm understanding, and responding to both the content and emotions of the speaker.


### Q3. What are the obstacles in your listening process?

- Distractions from my own thoughts  


### Q4. What can you do to improve your listening?

- Avoid getting lost in your own thoughts and try to stay focused when someone is speaking.  



### Q5. When do you switch to a Passive communication style in your day-to-day life?

- When talking to elderly people, people in senior positions, or when talking with strangers and meeting new people.  


### Q6. When do you switch into an Aggressive communication style in your day-to-day life?

- Mostly with people I am friendly with, especially when they annoy me.  


### Q7. When do you switch into Passive-Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day-to-day life?

- When talking with friends, to say things that are too blunt to say straight away.  


### Q8. How can you make your communication assertive? You can watch and analyze the videos, then think about what steps you can apply in your own life. (Watch the videos first before answering this question.)

- By recognizing and naming your feelings and needs, and then practicing in low-stakes situations.

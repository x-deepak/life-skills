#### Question 2. How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A and P.

For any behavior (B) to happen, three things must occur at once: Motivation, Ability, and a Prompt. If a behavior isn't happening, one of these is missing

#### Question 3. Why it is important to "Shine" or Celebrate after each successful completion of habit? (This is the most important concept in today's topic. Whatever you celebrate becomes a habit)

It's important to celebrate after the successful completion of a habit because the brain treats it as a reward, and the brain chases rewards.

#### Question 4. In this video, what was the most interesting story or idea for you?

Optimizing for the "starting line" rather than the "finish line." - To overcome the friction of starting, scale down any habit to a version that takes less than two minutes.

#### Question 5. What is the book's perspective about Identity?

It says that true long-term behavior change is identity change. Most people try to change their outcomes or actions, but the most effective way to build a habit is to focus on who you want to become.

#### Question 7. Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do?
